Overview

Perspectives & The Dolly Zoom

Become friends with your camera and every pixel it captures. In this project, you’ll build intuition for how perspective and focal length (zoom) interact with the center of projection to change how we perceive a scene. You’ll compare two portrait techniques, explore architectural “compression,” and recreate the classic dolly zoom.

Part 1

Selfie — The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Step back, zoom in, match face size — and watch perspective distortion disappear.

Tip: Keep the face the same size in both shots to isolate how distance + zoom affect perspective.

Part 2

Architectural Perspective Compression

Zoomed-in views “stack” distance; wide-angle views exaggerate depth.

Match overall scene size between images so the difference you perceive is largely perspective, not framing.

Part 3

The Dolly Zoom (“Vertigo Shot”)

Dolly the camera back while zooming in so your subject stays the same size but the background stretches.

Pro tip: Pick a scene with strong background lines (hallways, shelves, street lamps, stuffed animals) to make the effect read clearly.
